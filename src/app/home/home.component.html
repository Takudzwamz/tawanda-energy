Services

Fuelling a cleaner future

Tawanda Energy will create a decentralized, nutrient and energy hubs by converting end of life waste into energy and will empower local communities to leverage their waste to power positive change. Using smart business models, we do this in a way that is financially attractive today and is a small-scale, high impact approach that is revolutionizing the way we deal with waste in Zimbabwe.

Inspired by the values of the circular economy, we leverage the value of residual waste as an instrument of positive change by transforming it into new products; green energy, natural fertilizer and clean water. We do all of this on-site, at the two waste water treatment plants in Sakubva and Yeovil on a 25 year lease with the city of Mutare. Additionally, we will also work with agents who will go around the city collecting plastic waste to give a holistic approach to waste management in the city.

Tawanda Energy is entering a niche market; Municipal solid waste, sewage sludge, agricultural waste has not been recycled on any sizeable scale in Mutare or in Zimbabwe. This is a unique and viable concept that addresses the needs of various customers, stakeholders and reaches an untapped market with tremendous growth potential.

Areas we service

Tawanda’s headquarters are located in the City of Mutare; where from the past 5 years of its existence has been converting two waste water treatment plants into biorefineries. The concept of a bio-refinery is the sustainable processing of biomass into a spectrum of marketable products and energy.

It is an economy based on innovative and cost efficient use of waste for the production of both bio-based products and bio-energy. A bio-refinery is analogous to the modern day oil refinery where multiple products are produced from crude oil. All our activities are complimented, enabled and strengthened by smart partnerships with like-minded enterprises throughout the country.

Our Vision & Mission

Our vision is to be a driving force for social, environmental, and economic benefits by producing gaseous and liquid climate-neutral energy carriers and are a project that aims to accelerate the development and introduction of climate neutral fuels into the Zimbabwean transport, construction, farming, and energy sector.

FAQ's

What is waste to energy?
WtE is a proven, environmentally sound process that provides reliable electricity generation and sustainable disposal of post-recycling of sewage sludge and Municipal Solid Waste.

New Policies

New policies to encourage WTE can also have a meaningful impact in reducing dependence on fossil fuels and increasing production of renewable energy. This will be a clean and innovative waste disposal solutions that transform all carbon based waste into fuels such as oil, gas and green coal
What is a bio-refinery?
A bio-refinery is the sustainable processing of biomass into a spectrum of marketable products and energy. An economy based on innovative and cost efficient use of waste for the production of both bio-based products and bio-energy should be driven by well-developed integrated bio-refining systems.

Types of waste for the bio-refinery

The Mutare bio-refinery will use all kinds of biomass from forestry, agriculture, aquaculture, and residues from industry and households including sewage sludge, carbon based material, plastic, used tyres, waste paper, used oil, household waste, slaughter residues, textile,
Why use waste as our resource
By using waste as our feedstock the bio-refinery will not compete with any food source. Making waste a tradable commodity and when MSW becomes a tradable commodity, not only do businesses, individuals and families benefit from monetization, but it also opens the doors for the proper management of waste.

Using technology such as blockchain technology it will be possible for community to buy, sell, donate or exchange of waste generated from various human activities.
Components of the bio-refinery
Based on the above analysis, the bio-refinery project in Mutare mainly includes the following two classes of waste types (as projected according to the volume of generation in 2020):

• Municipal solid waste generated by the city on a daily basis 147 tonnes per day. MSW includes the following food and food processing waste, plastics, fat, oil and grease. It also includes solid waste already disposed at the uncontrolled landfills around the city.

• Waste water received at the waste water treatment plant on a daily basis: 60 t/d of dried sewage sludge. We will also add animal waste, agricultural waste and brewing waste.

In addition, the municipal sludge generated by the municipal WWTP can be incorporated into the scope of the Integrated Solid Waste Management Project and the municipal sludge generation amount in the urban center is 147 tonnes a day. The 147 tonnes of sewage sludge and the over 33 000 cubic metres of water are received at three different waste water treatment plants in the City of Mutare.
Community Integration
Perhaps the most significant barrier to the success of any WTE project is community approval and participation in the collection of MSW a feedstock for biofuels. A widespread information distribution plan to get community members and representatives on board with the proposal is necessary.

In order to further increase educational and outreach possibilities and turn the potential WTE plant into a resource for the community, a plan to engage the community so that the positive effects of the WTE plant will reach beyond the environmental and economic spheres and into the human one will be incorporated.
Source Separation
Source separation is seen as a critical element when constructing an integrated waste management programme and forms part of the re-use, recovery and recycles activities of the programme. By separating waste at source for recycling, it minimises the need for waste to go to landfill.

The proposed peer to peer waste collection system will provide a sustainable business platform that encourages households, businesses, institutions, and individuals to take part in separating waste at source.

The client will launch a campaign to encourage pilot source separation in five key suburbs in the City. An incentive of between US$0.05 - USS$0.10/kg of dry municipal solid waste will be offered to encourage community participation.
Waste generated in the City of Mutare
Approximately 60% of the waste generated in the city of Mutare is organic waste, excluding organic waste classified as construction and demolition waste. The amount of solid waste generated in Mutare is at 2.857 kg per day per household on average during the 2017 Mutare City waste survey. The total amount of waste produced was at 49.9 kg per annum (EMA, 2017).
